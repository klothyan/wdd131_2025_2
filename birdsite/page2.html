Common Birds of Rexburg

American Robin – A familiar songbird with a bright orange-red breast. Robins are known for their cheerful songs, often heard at dawn.

American Goldfinch – A small, bright yellow bird often seen fluttering through open fields.

Black-Capped Chickadee – A tiny, curious bird with a black cap and bib. They are highly intelligent and can store food for later.

Red-Winged Blackbird – Males have striking red and yellow shoulder patches. They are commonly found in wetlands.

European Starling – Introduced from Europe, starlings are highly adaptable and can mimic human speech.

House Sparrow – A small, social bird often found around human dwellings.

Mourning Dove – Known for their soft, mournful cooing. They are graceful, fast flyers that often feed on the ground.

House Finch – Males have a reddish-orange hue on their head and chest. They are known for their sweet, warbling songs.

Black-Billed Magpie – Intelligent and social birds often seen in groups. They are part of the crow family.
