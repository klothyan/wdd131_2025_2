Common Birds of Rexburg

American Robin – A familiar songbird with a bright orange-red breast. Robins are known for their cheerful songs, often heard at dawn. They primarily feed on worms, insects, and berries.

American Goldfinch – A small, bright yellow bird often seen fluttering through open fields. They have a distinctive, bouncy flight pattern and love to eat sunflower and thistle seeds.

Black-Capped Chickadee – A tiny, curious bird with a black cap and bib. They are highly intelligent and can store food for later. Their "chick-a-dee-dee-dee" call is iconic.

Red-Winged Blackbird – Males have striking red and yellow shoulder patches. They are commonly found in wetlands, where they make loud, melodic calls.

European Starling – Introduced from Europe, starlings are highly adaptable and can mimic human speech. Their glossy, iridescent feathers shimmer in the sunlight.

House Sparrow – A small, social bird often found around human dwellings. Originally from Europe, they have successfully adapted to cities and towns worldwide.

Mourning Dove – Known for their soft, mournful cooing. They are graceful, fast flyers that often feed on the ground in open fields.

House Finch – Males have a reddish-orange hue on their head and chest. They are known for their sweet, warbling songs.

Black-Billed Magpie – Intelligent and social birds often seen in groups. They are part of the crow family and can recognize themselves in mirrors.
